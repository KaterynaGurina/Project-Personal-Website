About Me

My name is Kateryna Gurina, and I’m a first-year biophysics undergraduate with a minor in computer science at Concordia University. I have a deep interest in the intersection of physics, computer science, and how they can be applied to solve real-world problems.

Currently, I’m exploring topics like quantum biology from the side of my major and full-stack development from the side of my minor. As the president of the Concordia Undergraduate Biochemistry, Chemistry, and Physics Society (CUBCAPS), I actively organize events to connect students with research opportunities and foster collaboration.

Outside of academics, I enjoy practicing Muay Thai, playing piano (classical mostly), and spending quality time with my dog Levi and cat Mochie (he happens to be the logo of this website that you see in the top left corner). I’m also passionate about traveling, exploring new cultures, and trying different cuisines!
